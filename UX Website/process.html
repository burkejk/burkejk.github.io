01

Discovery and Research

The discovery and research phases are critical steps in the UX process. Discovery encompasses reviewing or developing the project goal and evaluating the existing product while research includes user research and competitive analysis. It's during this phase that a designer uses surveys and user interviews to get to know the user and creates a persona to represent the target user.

02

Design

The design phase incorporates the research and focuses on the functionality and usablity of the site. During this phase a designer will develop the information architecture with site maps, wireframes and prototypes to display the design and to share with stakeholders.

03

Testing

Testing allows a designer to get feedback from users and incorporate this feedback into the design. This can be done in person or through remote user testing online.

04

Implementation

Once a design has been tested and gone through multiple iterations it is ready to be released. The initial release may contain only a few features with more added and improved upon over time.
